# Technology is both beneficial and harmful
### Technology is beneficial
Technology makes it easier than at any time in history to communicate with others and live our lives meaningfully. Search engines and personalized algorithms present us with infinite useful, good-faith information customized to our unique personal needs and interests. The internet enables us to comfortably socialize with family and friends at any distance, making it effortless to be socially connected. Digital tools take meaningless but necessary tasks that used to require large amounts of time and automates them, making them require our attention for far less time, giving us more time for more meaningful parts of life.

### Technology is harmful
Technology makes it easier than at any time in history to isolate ourselves and waste away our lives meaninglessly. Search engines and personalized algorithms present us with infinite divisive, bad-faith, emotionally-compelling ragebait customized to our unique personal insecurities. The internet enables us to comfortably replace real socialization with feeds and bots, making it effortless to be socially isolated. Digital tools take meaningless but enjoyable pastimes that used to remain entertaining for small amounts of time and automates them, making them keep our attention for far more time, giving us less time for more meaningful parts of life.	

# To minimize the harmful capabilities of technology without limiting its beneficial capabilities, you must use digital controls

### Why self control fails

Say someone has an internet addiction. 

Unlike some addictions, which may cost money, require the addict to drive to a store, or in some other way require some effort prior to indulgence, technology is always sitting in the addicts pocket, instantly accessible with no effort.

*A smoker will have a hard time quitting if there is always a pack of cigarettes in his pocket.*

One common recommendation for internet addicts is to delete social media apps, or use their phone's built in screen time settings. Yet these only add a several-second barrier. 
- Searching for a site in a browser takes seconds. 
- Re-downloading an app takes seconds. 
- Bypassing screen time settings via a four-digit passcode takes seconds. 

*A smoker will have a hard time quitting if cigarettes are always in a pocket that takes seconds to open.*

### Why digital controls succeed

Digital problems are uniquely well-suited to having digital solutions. 

With technology always in your pocket, relying on notoriously unreliable self-control will result in you quitting an technology addiction as easily as a smoker trying to quit who always keeps a pack of cigarettes in his pocket.

*A smoker will find it far easier to quit if throws his cigarettes out, putting a real barrier between himself and his addiction.*

These digital controls put a real barrier between you and the negative side of technology (whatever that means to you), without limiting your access to the positive side of technology.
